# Professional Vision and Career Goals

## How I Want to Grow

As I continue my career, I would like to become more involved in defining the direction of projects and features. Building features and writing code is something I enjoy, but I also believe I have good intuition for user needs and practical business requirements.

I enjoy thinking about the bigger picture and how a product should evolve. Because of this, I would like to gradually take on more responsibility in feature planning, product discussions, and project direction.

I also enjoy working directly with clients, stakeholders, and product owners. I believe one of my strengths is understanding both technical and non-technical perspectives. I like listening carefully, analyzing problems, and finding practical solutions that work for everyone involved.

Mentoring is another area I would like to develop further. Helping someone learn and improve is very rewarding, and I enjoy sharing knowledge and experience with others.

---

## What Makes a Project Successful

For me, the best projects are the ones where everyone benefits.

I like projects that create a win-win-win situation for clients, managers, and developers. When a product grows, solves real problems, and creates value for everyone involved, the work becomes much more meaningful.

A successful project is not only about delivering features. It is also about building something useful, sustainable, and enjoyable to work on.

---

## The Type of Work I Enjoy Most

I enjoy building new products and developing new features. There is something very satisfying about creating something from an idea, making it work, and seeing it provide value.

I also enjoy working with data. Data processing, transformation, visualization, and analysis are activities that make me feel comfortable and engaged. I like understanding information, finding patterns, and turning raw data into something useful.

In general, I enjoy variety. I like being involved in different parts of a project and doing whatever is needed to help move it forward.

---

## Work That I Find Less Motivating

The type of work I find least satisfying is repetitive testing where the same functionality must be verified repeatedly with small variations.

Testing is important and necessary, but repeatedly performing nearly identical actions without creating something new is not the part of software development that motivates me the most.

---

## General Approach to Expertise

I do not want to become extremely specialized in only one area, but I also do not want to stay at a surface level across many areas.

I prefer a balance between breadth and depth.

I like learning new technologies and skills, but I also believe there is great value in spending enough time with a technology or domain to truly make it your own. My goal is to build a wide range of capabilities while developing deep expertise in several important areas.

---

## Leadership and Responsibility

I see myself taking on greater responsibility in the future.

This does not necessarily mean traditional management, but I would like to contribute more to technical decisions, project direction, mentoring, and helping teams succeed.

I am ambitious and motivated by opportunities to make a larger impact.

---

## What Matters Most in My Next Role

Team culture and interesting work are the two most important factors for me.

When people work well together and enjoy solving meaningful problems, many other things naturally fall into place.

After that, remote work is important because I live in the countryside and enjoy the lifestyle my family has built there.

Stability and salary are also important considerations.

I view flexibility as part of a healthy team culture. Learning is also something I consider a natural part of being a software developer rather than a separate benefit.

---

## Industries and Domains That Interest Me

The domains that currently interest me the most are:

* Artificial Intelligence
* Smart Home Technologies
* Developer Tools

I enjoy working on products that combine practical value with interesting technical challenges.

---

## Projects I Would Like to Build

One project I would enjoy building someday is a CRM platform for restaurants or hotels.

Before becoming a software developer, I spent many years working as a chef and in the hospitality industry. Building software for that domain would allow me to combine my technical experience with the industry where I spent a large part of my professional life.

---

## Long-Term Professional Identity

If people know me professionally ten years from now, I would like them to say:

"Sashko understands his colleagues, clients, and their problems. He finds solutions, helps people, gives honest advice, takes responsibility, and does not waste time making excuses."

That is the professional reputation I would like to earn.

---

## Long-Term Career Direction

In the future, I can see myself moving closer to product ownership while remaining connected to the technical side of software development.

I enjoy understanding users, defining requirements, discussing solutions, and helping shape products. Product ownership feels like a natural extension of many of the things I already enjoy doing.

---

## Definition of Success

For me, success is not only professional.

In life, success means growing older and having children and grandchildren who genuinely want to spend time with you.

In my career, success means giving my best effort every day, continuing to learn throughout my life, and sharing knowledge with other people whenever I can.
