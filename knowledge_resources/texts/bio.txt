# Personal Background

## Early Life (1987–1999)

I was born in 1987 in Sofia, Bulgaria, in a normal middle-class family. I grew up with one sister. My childhood was very happy and active, especially in the 1990s. Most of my time was spent playing outside with friends from the neighborhood, often until late in the evening.

I loved football and trained regularly when I was a child. I also practiced sport gymnastics. My school was located in the same neighborhood where I grew up, so my daily life, friends, and school environment were all closely connected.

During the summers, I often went with my cousins to our family villa near Sofia, where my grandparents stayed. These summers were very important in my life because they created strong family memories and shaped my connection to music and family traditions.

---

## Family Environment

I grew up in a very close and connected extended family. We spent a lot of time together with grandparents, uncles, aunts, cousins, and close family friends. I had one sister and also two close female cousins that were part of my everyday environment.

Music was an important part of our family life. My grandfather played professional guitar when he was young, and my father and uncle also played music. When the whole family gathered at the villa, we often played instruments, sang, listened to music, and danced. This is where my strong emotional connection to music comes from.

Our family has always been fans of Levski Sofia. I trained in the club when I was young, and today I am still a big fan of the team and of football in general.

---

## School and Education

In primary school, I was a strong student with very high grades. My family was serious and demanding about education, so I grew up with strong expectations regarding learning and discipline.

In high school, where I studied optics and electronics, I started to put less effort into school. Near the end of high school, I began taking extra mathematics lessons to prepare for university exams.

I later started university studying ecology, but I did not finish it. Instead, I made a major life change and started working as a chef, which became the focus of my life for around 15 years.

---

## Cooking Career (15 Years)

I started my career as a chef in Sofia. Over time, I took on more responsibilities in each restaurant I worked in.

I worked in different types of kitchens, including traditional Bulgarian restaurants, Italian restaurants, fusion cuisine, and burger houses. I also spent two summers working at the Bulgarian seaside. Later I moved to Veliko Tarnovo and worked there as well. After that, I worked as a chef in a small villa (guest house) in Apriltsi, in a forest area.

One of my strongest achievements during this time was learning how to take the best from each colleague and also share my own experience with others.

The hardest part of this lifestyle was working every night, every holiday, and every weekend, while my friends and family had a completely different schedule.

When my wife and I decided to have a child, I made the decision to end my cooking career.

---

## Transition into Technology

I started coding at the end of the COVID lockdown period. I began with courses at SoftUni while still working as a chef. This period was very intensive because I was combining full-time work with studying.

I learned Python, web development with JavaScript and HTML, and databases. I also learned Django, PostgreSQL, and single-page applications. My diploma project was my first larger application called “Fishbook”.

During this time, I was also working on multiple life changes at once: studying, working as a chef, searching for a new career, and repairing a house for my family.

Eventually, I found an opportunity to leave the cooking industry and move into a computer-based job. It was not a programmer position, but it allowed me to step out of the kitchen profession.

After that transition, I started an internship at Infinno LLC. After completing the internship, I continued working there, and this is currently my job.

---

## Family Life and Current Situation

My wife is a very important part of my life story. She was the main reason I moved from Sofia to Veliko Tarnovo, because she was studying there at the time. I decided that I wanted to live with her, because I believe that long-distance relationships are not the right choice for me.

She has always supported me in every important decision. I trust her completely, and that trust is a central part of our relationship. We spent our late 20s and early 30s living a free and active lifestyle together. Over time, we grew together and became more focused on family life, our child, and maintaining meaningful relationships with close friends and family.

We have a daughter. She is exactly what a child is supposed to be. Sometimes we lose patience and expect her to behave like an adult, but we always realize that what she needs most is love and understanding. Becoming a father gave me more responsibility, but also more confidence. It gave me less sleep but more laughter. Watching my child grow and develop is one of the most important parts of my life.

We currently live in a village between Lovech, Troyan, and Apriltsi. It is a very beautiful and calm natural environment. We have a house, a garden where we grow vegetables, several trees, and grape vines. The house is old, and I have repaired most of it together with help from my father. We are still working on improving both the house and the yard, but we prefer preserving the old style as much as possible.

We both work from home. Our child goes to kindergarten. We often visit my parents in Sofia or my wife’s parents in Apriltsi.

---

## Lifestyle and Interests

In my current life, I value living in a quiet and clean environment. I like having enough space for my family, as well as space for gardening and outdoor life.

My hobbies include fishing, skiing, tennis, DIY repairs, gardening, and camping.

---

## Personality and Values

I prefer not to strictly define my personality, because I believe people are better understood through their actions than their own description of themselves. I try to be calm and positive in general. I am somewhat introverted, but I am social with the right people. One of my strongest traits is that I am very focused and persistent when I set goals.

The most important values in my life are family, friendship, raising children to be good people, keeping nature clean, and honesty.
